About one-fifth of the world's seaborne oil and LNG trade transits the Strait of Hormuz. Since the United States and Israel launched airstrikes on Iran on 28 February 2026, outbound shipping has been repeatedly and severely disrupted — first by Iran's closure of the Strait, then by a US naval blockade, a brief recovery after the 17 June memorandum of understanding, and renewed disruption since. All figures are index values (2025 daily average ≈ 100), not shipment counts. Data source: WTO Data Lab — Strait of Hormuz Trade Tracker.

Methodology. Indices are derived from AIS (Automatic Identification System) vessel-tracking data compiled by AXSMarine for the WTO Data Lab. Values reflect voyages actively transmitting AIS; if a vessel disables or loses AIS transmission while transiting the Strait, that voyage will not be captured — so recorded volumes may understate true traffic, especially during the conflict when vessels reportedly went dark to avoid detection. The 7-day moving average smooths day-to-day volatility; the prior-year series compares each date to the same calendar day one year earlier.
